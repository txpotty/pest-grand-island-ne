Discovering Physical Silverfish

The most obvious sign of a silverfish infestation is seeing the actual insects. Silverfish are small, wingless insects, typically about half an inch to an inch long. They have a teardrop shape and move in a wiggling, fish-like motion, which is how they get their name. They are often silver or grayish in color, although some can be brown or black. You might spot them scurrying across floors, walls, or counters, especially in the dark hours after you turn on a light. Seeing one silverfish could indicate the presence of many more hidden away. These sightings are usually concentrated in humid areas, like bathrooms, kitchens, basements, and attics. It's important not to underestimate their ability to reproduce quickly once they find a suitable environment.

The Life Cycle of a Silverfish

Knowing the silverfish life cycle can provide insights into why controlling them can be challenging. Silverfish go through a process called gradual metamorphosis. They hatch from eggs as nymphs, which resemble smaller versions of the adult silverfish. These nymphs molt numerous times as they grow, potentially living for several years under favorable conditions. Unlike many insects, silverfish continue to molt even after reaching adulthood. This long lifespan and continuous molting can contribute to persistent infestations if not properly addressed. Their ability to live for several years means that a small initial problem can grow significantly over time if left untreated.
