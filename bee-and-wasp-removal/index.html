Signs You Need Professional Bee and Wasp Removal

Sometimes it's not immediately obvious that you have a serious bee or wasp problem. Knowing the signs can help you determine when it's time to call in the professionals. Ignoring a potential infestation can lead to it growing larger and becoming more dangerous. It's always better to address the issue early on.

Increased Stinging Insect Activity

One of the most apparent signs is a significant increase in the number of bees or wasps around your property. If you notice a constant stream of these insects flying in and out of a particular area, it's a strong indication of a nearby nest. This is especially concerning if they are congregating near areas where people or pets frequent, like patios, playgrounds, or entryways. Don't underestimate the danger of a growing colony; even a small number of stinging insects can pose a threat, particularly to individuals with allergies.

Visible Nests or Hives

Seeing a visible nest or hive is a clear sign that professional help is needed. Bee hives can range in size and are often found in protected areas like walls, trees, or under eaves. Wasp nests, such as those of paper wasps, yellowjackets, or hornets, also vary in appearance and location. Attempting to remove a nest yourself can be extremely dangerous and is not recommended. Stinging insects will aggressively defend their homes, and disturbing a nest without proper protection and knowledge can lead to multiple painful stings. Our team has the expertise and equipment to safely remove nests of all sizes and in various locations.

Buzzing Sounds Within Walls or Ceilings

If you hear persistent buzzing or scratching sounds within your walls, ceilings, or attic, it could indicate a hive or nest is present inside the structure of your home. These sounds can become more prominent during warmer months when the insects are most active. Having a nest inside your home is a serious concern, not only because of the stinging risk but also potential structural damage from the nest itself, fluids produced by the colony, or even mold growth. Locating and removing nests within structures requires specialized techniques and equipment to avoid causing damage to your property during the process. Our technicians are trained to handle these types of challenging removals safely and efficiently.

Unusual Behavior in Pets or Family Members

Your pets or family members might exhibit unusual behavior, such as avoiding certain areas of the yard or house, if they have encountered bees or wasps. This could be a subtle sign that there's a hidden nest nearby that has gone unnoticed. Pay attention to where your pets are hesitant to go or if you observe any unexplained anxieties when they are outdoors. Children, especially, might express fear or concern about certain parts of the yard, which could be a result of encountering stinging insects. Taking these cues seriously and investigating further can help you identify a potential problem before it escalates. Learn more about residential pest control.

Discovering Stings

If you or someone in your household has been stung, it's a direct indication that stinging insects are present on your property. While a single sting might be a random occurrence, multiple stings or stings occurring frequently should raise a red flag. Understanding that bees and wasps are protective of their territories and nests makes encountering stings a strong indicator of a nearby colony. Don't ignore this warning sign; addressing the source of the stings is crucial for preventing future incidents and ensuring the safety of everyone on your property. Common Places Where Bees and Wasps Build Nests in Grand Island

Bees and wasps are resourceful builders and can establish nests in a variety of locations around your home and property. Understanding these common nesting sites can help you identify potential problem areas and know where to look for signs of an infestation. Being aware of these locations can prompt you to act quickly if you suspect activity.

Under Eaves and Overhangs

Eaves and overhangs are popular nesting sites for paper wasps and certain types of hornets. The protected nature of these areas offers shelter from the elements and provides a stable surface for nest attachment. Nests built in these locations are often visible and can grow quite large, especially as the colony expands throughout the spring and summer months. Their proximity to high-traffic areas around your home makes them particularly hazardous. These nests are usually made of a papery material, which gives paper wasps their name. It's not uncommon to find multiple small nests in close proximity under eaves.

Attics and Wall Voids

Attics and wall voids provide dark, secluded, and protected spaces that are ideal for certain bee and wasp species, such as yellowjackets and honey bees. Nests established within these structural elements can be difficult to access and remove, often requiring specialized equipment and techniques. The presence of a nest within walls can also make those buzzing sounds we discussed earlier. Removing nests from these locations is particularly important due to the potential for structural damage, staining from nest materials and waste, and the difficulty of containing the insects within your home during removal. Our technicians are experienced in safely accessing and removing nests from these challenging locations, minimizing disruption to your home. We also offer rodent control which often involves inspecting attics and wall voids.

Sheds, Garages, and Outbuildings

Sheds, garages, and other outbuildings offer plenty of sheltered spaces for bees and wasps to build nests. Rafters, corners, and even stored items within these structures can become home to a colony. Infestations in these areas can make it difficult to access and use your outbuildings safely. The presence of tools and equipment within sheds and garages can also add another layer of complication to removal efforts. Our team is skilled in safely removing nests from various types of outbuildings, allowing you to use these spaces without worry. Looking for pest control in Aurora? We can help.

Trees and Shrubs

Trees and shrubs, especially those with dense foliage, can provide excellent hiding places for bee and wasp nests. Some species, like bald-faced hornets, build large, conspicuous nests hanging from tree branches. Others may establish nests in cavities within trees or within thick bushes. Nests in vegetation can be particularly challenging to spot and remove due to the surrounding plant life. Disturbing a nest in a tree or shrub can be very dangerous, especially if you are attempting to prune the vegetation. Professional removal ensures that the nest is located and eliminated safely without putting yourself at risk. We also help with eliminating pests on trees and shrubs like certain ant species.

Ground Nests

Certain wasp species, like yellowjackets, commonly build nests underground. These nests are often located in old rodent burrows, abandoned ant hills, or other cavities in the soil. Ground nests can be particularly dangerous because they are often hidden and can be easily disturbed by mowing, gardening, or simply walking across the area. The inhabitants of ground nests are known for their aggressive defense when their nest is threatened. Identifying a ground nest can be difficult, often only revealed by observing wasps flying in and out of a small hole in the ground. These areas provide protection from the weather and are often undisturbed, allowing colonies to grow unnoticed.
